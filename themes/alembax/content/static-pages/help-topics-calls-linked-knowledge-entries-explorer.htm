Linked Knowledge Entries Explorer Option

You can view a list of the knowledge bank articles linked to the current call. These are not specifically attached to the call like an object, but recorded to the database as a ‘referral’, or link, from a call to a specific knowledge entry. A call can have any number of referrals to knowledge entries.

  1. Display the Call Details window, if it is not already on screen.
  2. In the Explorer pane, expand Resolution, then select Linked Knowledge Entries.

    3. An orange box appears alongside the Linked Knowledge Entries if articles are linked to the current call.

  3. Click to display more columns in the browse table, if not all are visible
    Entity Type The type of knowledge article; such as Abstract or FAQ
    Title Title of the knowledge article
    Ref The knowledge article's reference number
    Status Status of the knowledge article, such as Internal, Published, or Moderated
    Thumbnail A sample of the knowledge article contents
    Resolved By

    Read only. Indicates which of the linked knowledge articles was used to resolve the call. This check box becomes ticked when a knowledge article is selected and is pressed in the About the Matching Panel or the Linked Knowledge Entries window.
  4. Perform an action as needed:
    • double-click a row to open an article.
    • click to open an article
    • click to remove the link between the call and a knowledge article.
    • click to perform a knowledge search
    • click to save the details of the current call as a knowledge article.
    • click to save the details of the current call to a bulletin board article
    • click to link a knowledge article to the child calls of the current call
    • click to link a knowledge article and immediately close the current call.

    • On selecting , you may be prompted to enter outage information; only do so if you wish to create an open outage for that linked CMDB item.

      If a call was resolved by a knowledge article and then reopened, selecting on a different knowledge will not switch this flag to the new article. It is recommended to remove the link to the previous knowledge article.
